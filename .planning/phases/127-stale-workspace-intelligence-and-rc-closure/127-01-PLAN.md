## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|---|---|
| Persisted provenance → provider runner | Untrusted provider/host/repository/change metadata selects argv for an external process. |
| Git/provider/worktree observations → stale policy | Fallible evidence could be confused with a confirmed cleanup reason. |
| Concurrent probes/cache → revisioned response | Late or stale work could overwrite newer authoritative evidence. |
| Evaluator → lifecycle authority | Advisory evaluation must not gain mutation capabilities. |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-127-01-01 | Tampering / Elevation | Forge argv construction | high | mitigate | RED tests require complete validated provenance, fixed executable/argument positions, encoding, and shell-free runner use. |
| T-127-01-02 | Information Disclosure | Provider and projection results | high | mitigate | Disclosure canaries require fixed unknown enums and exclude paths, credentials, environment, argv, stdout, and stderr. |
| T-127-01-03 | Tampering | Revision/cache generations | high | mitigate | Deterministic deferred probes prove revision-before-probe and newest-generation-only cache commits. |
| T-127-01-04 | Denial of Service | Probe fan-out | medium | mitigate | Tests enforce bounded concurrency, timeout, output cap, abort, singleflight, and atomic settled responses. |
| T-127-01-05 | Spoofing | Unknown evidence | high | mitigate | Closed three-state fixtures require a confirmed positive reason and isolate unknown-only rows. |
| T-127-01-06 | Elevation of Privilege | Evaluator capability graph | high | mitigate | Hostile mutation sentinels fail any attempt to invoke lifecycle, worktree, YAML, or provider mutation. |

**ASVS L1 gate:** every high-severity threat and every medium threat on these primary boundaries must have a green mitigation test; any unresolved high-severity item blocks execution of dependent plans.
