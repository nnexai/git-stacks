## Bounded scope and commit rationale

This plan deliberately keeps the 12-file service observation -> bounded contract/fixture -> strict native decoder -> pure projection chain atomic. Splitting that chain across independently schedulable plans would permit an intermediate service wire shape that the native model cannot decode, or a native projection compiled against fixture fields the service does not yet emit. The atomic plan is the bounded exception; execution remains manageable through three ordered, focused commits with non-overlapping ownership:

1. Task 1 owns the five-file service observation/projection slice and commits only after its focused Bun suites pass.
2. Task 2 owns the five-file contract-fixture/native-decoder slice and commits only after the contract round-trip and native service-client suites pass.
3. Task 3 owns the two-file GTK-free projection slice and commits only after workspace UI goldens pass.

Do not combine task commits or begin a downstream task before the prior focused verification succeeds. Treat those commits as internal recovery checkpoints, not independently deployable boundaries: the plan may be merged/exposed only after all three tasks pass, so no intermediate wire/model shape escapes the atomic plan. This keeps each executor context to at most five modified files without weakening end-to-end compatibility.
